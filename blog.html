My Thoughts

  • 1. Subspace Clustering is quite interesting when we think about assigning different feature weights vector to different subspace. If each of human emotions comprises of certain "features", a same feature will play a different degree of importance in different emotions. If we think subspace clustering as assigning not different feature weights vector, but different feature disparse or structure or any artificial unit vector, there is a possibility that we can define our emotion as a more well-rounded model. In other words, the way to define our real world does not rely on "how we deal with the data", or "algorithm" but "how we look at the data", or "data structure".
